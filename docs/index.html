FAQQuestions

What is Wakala?
Wakala is an agency contract: the customer appoints the bank as agent to invest or act on their behalf for a fee. Returns belong to the principal; the agent earns a disclosed fee rather than interest.
What business area does Wakala Investment Account belong to?
Wakala Investment Account sits in the Deposits and Investment Accounts business area — Shariah-compliant deposit and investment accounts, where returns come from profit-sharing and safekeeping rather than interest. It follows the BIAN Fulfill functional pattern over the Wakala Investment Account asset, with the Wakala Investment Account Fulfillment Arrangement as its control record.
How is this isolated from conventional banking?
Completely. The Islamic landscape shares no code, repositories, namespaces or events with the conventional platform. This domain lives in the isb-deposits namespace with an isb-* repository and com.bank.islamic package, so the two can never mix by accident.
Is this a certified Shariah-compliant product?
No. It is an educational reference implementation in the BIAN service-domain style. Actual Shariah compliance of any real deployment requires certification by a qualified Shariah Supervisory Board. Standards referenced: AAOIFI, IFSB, IIFM.
